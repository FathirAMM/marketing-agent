## 34. FINANCIAL RISK MANAGEMENT OBJECTIVES AND POLICIES

<|ref|>text<|/ref|><|det|>[[126, 375, 910, 431]]<|/det|>
The Group's principal financial liabilities comprise trade payables, amounts due to related parties and other payables. The main purpose of these financial liabilities is to finance the Group's operations. The Group's principal financial assets include bank balances and cash, short-term deposits, trade receivables, amounts due from related parties and other receivables that derive directly from its operations. The Group also holds equity securities.

<|ref|>text<|/ref|><|det|>[[126, 449, 875, 475]]<|/det|>
The Group is exposed to market risk, credit risk and liquidity risk. The Board of Directors reviews and agrees policies for managing each of these risks, which are summarised below.

<|ref|>sub_title<|/ref|><|det|>[[126, 494, 206, 503]]<|/det|>
### Market Risk

<|ref|>text<|/ref|><|det|>[[126, 509, 874, 535]]<|/det|>
Market risk is the risk that the fair value of future cash flows of a financial instrument will fluctuate because of changes in market prices. Market risk of the Group comprises interest rate risk, foreign currency risk and equity price risk.

<|ref|>sub_title<|/ref|><|det|>[[126, 554, 243, 563]]<|/det|>
### Interest Rate Risk

<|ref|>text<|/ref|><|det|>[[126, 569, 890, 610]]<|/det|>
Interest rate risk is the risk that the fair value or future cash flows of a financial instrument will fluctuate due to of changes in market interest rates. The Group's exposure to the risk of changes in market interest rates relates primarily to the Group's financial assets and liabilities with floating interest rates.
